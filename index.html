Z O N T U

Zontu develops AI-assisted solutions to prevent illicit poaching, regulate wildlife tourism, and reduce human-animal conflicts in wildlife parks. Animal behaviour data like images, location, and bodily vitals, are collected via sensors, which is processed in real-time to fulfil different user needs.

OUR STORY

ZONTU - AI for wildlife conservation

The team comprises 4 members hailing from Assam in India, who have the common interest for wildlife conservation in north-east India. 3 members (Eashan, Hridoy, Utpal) are postdocs at the Cambridge University and handle Zontu’s technical activities, while the last member (Ashapurna) is a legal expert, and she focuses on data-protection and wildlife acts. Eashan has a background in bioengineering, Hridoy specialises in applying AI technologies and IoT solutions, and Utpal is pursuing research in Computer Architecture.

Poaching, human-animal conflicts pose a major threat to endangered species globally. Between 2010-20, 190 rhinos were killed for their horns in Kaziranga National Park (KNP), Assam, while elephants were hunted for their tusks. Furthermore, indigenous communities around KNP face harassment from local authorities, and frequent eviction drives have flared social tensions. Zontu focuses on the diverse tropical wildlife of Northeastern India, which remains largely unexplored by modern ecologists. Zontu’s application allows real-time detection of poaching/stray incidents in real time. Moreover, long-term sensor data will allow monitoring of the adverse effects of climate change on animal behaviour in tropical forests.

The name Zontu is derived from the Assamese word for 'animal', and the venture is currently at the ideation stage. Zontu’s first product - a smartphone app for park rangers, displays heatmaps of last-known animal locations, identified from live stream feeds via small single-board computers installed at multiple checkpoints, thereby preventing accidents during tours and stray incidents. Zontu’s second product is a large-scale spatio-temporal sensor dataset (dataset or framework) to aid in the study of animal grouping, territoriality, and migratory behaviour.
